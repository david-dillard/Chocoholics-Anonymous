Glossary

1) ChocAn: abbreviated form of Chocoholics Anonymous.

2) Member name: the name of a member, stated on their ChocAn card.

3) Member number: a member’s unique nine digit number, stated on their ChocAn card.

4) Suspended member: a ChocAn member who has had their account suspended for not paying membership fees for at least one month.

5) Provider: health care professional.

6) Provider number: a provider’s unique nine digit number.

7) Service code: specific six digit code in the health care industry used to refer to a specific medical service.

8) Provider directory: an alphabetically ordered list of service names and associated service codes and fees.

9) ChocAn Data Center: stores and handles member and provider data, also the location of where ChocAn operators work.

10) ChocAn operator: an employee at the ChocAn Data Center who handles member and provider information.

11) ChocAn manager: an employee at the data center who can request the member, provider, and summary reports.

12) Main accounting procedure: reads the week’s file of services provided and prints the number of reports (including member, provider, and summary reports); run every Friday at the ChocAn Data center.

13) Acme Accounting Services: 3rd party organization who handles financial procedures relating to automated member payments for ChocAn.

14) Electronic funds transfer: often abbreviated EFT, refers to transferring funds from an electronic source.

15) Member report: a weekly document sent to members who consulted a ChocAn provider that week listing member name, member number, member street address, member city, member state, member ZIP code, and additionally for each service provided the following details are required: date of service, provider name, and service name.

16) Provider report: a weekly document sent to each provider who billed ChocAn during that week listing provider name, provider number, provider street address, provider city, provider state, provider ZIP code, total number of consultations with members, total fee for the week, and additionally for each service provided, the following details are required: date of service, date and time data were received by the computer, member name, member number, service code, and fee to be paid.

17) Summary report: sent to the ChocAn manager for accounts payable listing every provider to be paid that week, the number of consultations each had, and his or her total fee for that week.

Use Cases

Use case: Verify Member Card

Context: A ChocAn provider needs to verify a member’s card.

Actors: Provider, Member

Main Success Scenario:

1. A member hands their provider their ChocAn card.

2. The provider scans the card using the card reader on his or her terminal.

3. The member number on the card is validated by the ChocAn Data Center.

4. The word “Validated” is displayed on the terminal screen.

Extensions:

3a. If the member number is not valid, the reason (Invalid number, Member suspended, etc.) is displayed.

Use case: Run Accounting Procedure

Context:Friday at midnight, end of week reports must be generated and distributed to those who need them.

Actors: Operator

Main Success Scenario:

1. Reads file of all services provided for the week.

2. Each member who has consulted a ChocAn provider receives a list of all services provided for that week in the form of the member report.

3. Providers who billed ChocAn for a service provided receive a list of all the services they provided to ChocAn members in the form of a provider report.

4. All electronic funds transfer data is written to disk for later use in paying providers.

5. ChocAn managers receive summary reports on all providers and their services.

Extensions: N/A

Use case: Bill ChocAn for Services

Context: A provider wishes to bill ChocAn for a service

Actors: Provider

Main Success Scenario:

1. The provider scans the member’s card at the provider’s terminal.

2. The terminal displays “VALIDATED”.

3. The provider keys in the date in the format of MM-DD-YYYY.

4. The provider uses the Provider Directory to find the 6 digit code that corresponds to the service provided.

5. The provider keys in the 6 digit code to the terminal.

6. The terminal then displays the name of the service corresponding to the service code to ensure it was the correct code (up to 20 characters).

7. The provider verifies that this is the correct service that was provided and adds any additional comments about the service.

8. The software product writes a record to disk.

8.1. The record includes the following fields:

Current date and time (MM–DD–YYYY HH:MM:SS).

Date service was provided (MM–DD–YYYY).

Provider number (9 digits).

Member number (9 digits).

Service code (6 digits).

Comments (100 characters) (optional).

9. The software product then looks up and displays the fee to be paid on the provider’s terminal.

10. For verification purposes, the provider has a form to enter the current date and time, the date the service was provided, member name and number, service code, and fee to be paid.

11. At the end of the week, the provider totals the fees to verify the amount to be paid to that provider by ChocAn for that week.

Extensions:

1a. The member does not have their member card.

1a.1. The provider enters the member’s member number in the terminal.

1a.2. The use case continues at step 2.

6a. The terminal determined that the code was invalid.

6a.1. An error message is printed on the terminal.

6a.2. The use case continues at step 5.
